A Brief Psychohistory of Indian Politics-Delhi Elections

Nov 08 2014December 1, 2014
The upcoming Delhi election is a critical point in the course of Indian politics. This will decide whether we will have another national party in the next 10 to 15 years. After the Maharashtra and Haryana elections only three parties have the National party status, namely the BJP, Congress and CPI(M). Three parties lost their status which includes SP, BSP and CPI. With the CPI(M) losing base in Bengal. India is set for bipolar politics in the future.
There are basically three kinds of people in Delhi, pro BJP, anti BJP and the pseudo intellectual middle class . In the last Delhi elections the anti BJP votes got split between the Congress and AAP. Yet, AAP got around 28 seats by being able to sway the neutral middle class votes to their side.
With that little bit of anti BJP votes that were left, Congress managed a few seats. BJP bakths and a Modi wave made BJP the single largest party.
But this time the whole scenario has changed in Delhi. The anti BJP votes are consolidated and the Jadu button will be pressed by the consolidation till the button pops out. This is because of no special liking for the AAP or Kejriwal, but because they believe that Congress has no chance of putting up a fight against the BJP. This is a plus for the AAP. But, by dissolving the government in Delhi just after 49 days, the neutral middle class has been split into two groups. Some people saw the 49 days full of reforms and a goverment by the people. Some saw it as a marketing strategy to pitch themselves in the general elections. This time it is a battle between AAP and BJP, and this is not going to be an easy win for either party.
This election is crucial for AAP. Unlike BJP or the Congress, AAP is not organisationaly strong or doesn’t have any regional leaders(of some consequence). It is centered around Kejriwal. So, if AAP loses this election, it is pretty much the end of AAP. Congress will certainly come back strong in the next 5 years and the anti-BJP votes will be split.And people vote only for a winnable candidate, so the perspective of the voter about AAP changes. And with every election the vote percentage of AAP will reduce and it will simply cease to exist(or exist like CPI or MDMK).
But if AAP wins Delhi, it will be a big push for AAP in other states too. People will start seeing them as an alternative. And basically it will feed on the anti-BJP votes that belongs to the Congress.

If Modi and Amit Shah’s vision of Congress Mukth Bharat must come true, they must let AAP win this Delhi election.
